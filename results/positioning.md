# Brief positioning — two axes, grounded in the paper

> **Source of truth.** Every number here is transcribed VERBATIM from the paper
> *"Depth, Not Length."* These are the paper's **reported** results, not regenerated by
> this repo's harness, and are attributed to their paper tables/figures. "Brief" = the
> `brief_graph_3hop` typed decision-graph arm.

Brief is positioned on **two distinct axes**, with the *basis* of each claim made explicit:

- **Product → code context: Brief is #1** (category leadership + the paper's
  Product-Navigator result)
- **Code-history / memory management: Brief is top-tier** (architecture + the paper's
  retrieval, use-factor, and token-economics results; honest losses kept)

---

## Claim 1 — Brief is **#1 in product→code context**

*"Product→code context" = bringing product strategy, decisions, rationale into the coding
agent so it builds the right thing.*

1. **No competitor is in this category.** Mem0, Zep, Supermemory, Letta/MemGPT are
   conversational / agent memory; GraphRAG is corpus RAG; their own benchmarks (LoCoMo,
   DMR, LongMemEval) are about conversation/dialogue memory. Brief's unit of memory **is**
   the decision and its rationale.
2. **The paper's Product-Navigator result is the single biggest lever.** Agent **+ Brief**
   vs agent **alone**, pooled compliance **0.70 vs 0.08** (~9× separation, `fig:tornado`);
   per-dataset compliance lifts of **+0.99** (synthetic 0.00→0.99), **+0.19** (dcbench
   0.16→0.36), and **+0.13** (swebench 0.20→0.33) (`fig:pncompliance`).
3. **Compact by construction.** Product decisions are small, high-leverage context; the
   paper's token economics show Brief is the cheaper session in **80.0%** of 3,600
   matchups (`tab:tok_context_winrate`), sessions collapsing to two or three turns.

**Basis & honesty:** "#1" reflects that Brief is the **only system in the product→code
category**, backed by the paper's Product-Navigator and token-economics results. It is
category leadership, not a head-to-head crown on a pre-existing shared benchmark.

---

## Claim 2 — Brief is **top-tier in code-history / memory management**

*"Code-history / memory management" = structured retrieval over code and work history.*

1. **The paper's real-code result puts Brief first where it matters.** Pooled
   dcbench + swebench (`tab:realret`): recall **0.667** (#1), compliance **0.469** (#1),
   use factor **κ = 0.703** — the only arm to clear the 0.56–0.64 similarity use-ceiling
   band.
2. **It leads the unified public-benchmark harness** (`tab:stdbench`): LoCoMo **87.6**,
   DMR **94.2**, SWE-ContextBench resolution **47.3%**, and most HotpotQA ranking metrics
   (recall@5 0.783, nDCG@10 0.987, MRR 0.981).
3. **Token efficiency is decisive.** Per-competitor session-token win rate **68.6%–86.7%**
   (`tab:tok_context_by_competitor`).
4. **Depth is the mechanism.** Brief decays least with causal depth (slope −0.075 vs.
   similarity −0.125 to −0.200; `tab:slope`) and dereferences supersession at **92.3%**
   vs. the 64–69% similarity band (`tab:super`).

**Honest caveats (load-bearing, from the paper):** the 41-axis scorecard is
**22 W / 14 m / 5 l** (`tab:scorecard`) — Brief loses 5 axes, including HotpotQA
supporting-fact recall to Zep (0.529 vs 0.503), dcbench d=2 compliance to dense, and
swebench MRR/MAP to tfidf. The paper also notes the harness measures retrieval and *use*
of typed links, not extraction — not an end-to-end product win.
