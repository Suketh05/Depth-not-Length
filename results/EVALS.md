## Headline head-to-heads

All values are the paper's reported results (see [`results/METRICS.md`](METRICS.md) for
the full tables and CSV links).

### Real code, pooled (Brief vs. best similarity baseline) — `tab:realret`

### Synthetic compliance (Brief vs. field) — `tab:synthall`

Brief **0.933** vs. tfidf 0.883, bm25 0.875, dense 0.858, raptor 0.817, none 0.025.

+0.025 at d=1, +0.025 at d=2, **+0.075** at d=3; Brief decays least (slope −0.075 vs.
similarity −0.125 to −0.200).

### Supersession (current-decision-first %) — `tab:super`

Brief **92.3%** vs. the similarity band 64–69% (dense 68.7, tfidf 65.8, bm25 64.1);
chance = 50%.

### Public benchmarks — `tab:stdbench` / `tab:t101pub`

### Token economics — `tab:tok_context_winrate`

Brief is the cheaper session in **80.0%** of 3,600 matchups (0 ties), 68.6%–86.7% per
competitor.

## Scope (from the paper)

The harness supplies a common pre-extracted decision corpus, so it measures retrieval and
*use* of typed links, not extraction. The paper states this is not an end-to-end product
win, marks each claim as **proven / calibrated / under-identified**, and reads the
swebench d=3 cell (n=12) as under-powered.
